# Evidence triangulation

1. Convert the request into non-overlapping claim families and explicit evidence requirements.
2. Assign independent families concurrently. Reserve duplicate research only for high-risk facts that require corroboration.
3. Prefer primary sources, then add an independent source where the claim is material or disputed.
4. Normalize dates, currencies, units, identifiers, and definitions before comparing values.
5. Record disagreement as structured conflict: claim, source A, source B, likely cause, and whether it blocks the conclusion.
6. Audit every major conclusion back to evidence. Perform one bounded gap round for blocking omissions; otherwise stop.
