# Evidence policy

- Important factual claims need a direct source URL and an explicit as-of or publication date when time matters.
- Prefer official records, standards, filings, first-party documentation, research papers, and company or institution publications.
- Use secondary reporting for context and independent corroboration, not as a substitute for an available primary source.
- State units and calculation methods. Never merge figures with different dates, currencies, definitions, or contract bases.
- Mark inference as inference. Report material source disagreement and evidence gaps.
- Do not count search-result pages, invented URLs, local pseudo-URLs, or unreachable references as evidence.
